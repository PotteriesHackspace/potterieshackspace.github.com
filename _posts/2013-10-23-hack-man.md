A few weeks ago we started on a new project after acquiring an old 28&#8243; CRT monitor with VGA inputs.

**The Hackspace arcade machine.**

<span style="font-style: inherit; line-height: 1.625;">The model was designed Sketchup based on an original CAPCOM arcade cabinet.</span>

[<img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-369" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/arcade-cab.jpg?w=584" alt="arcade cab" width="584" height="264" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/arcade-cab.jpg 1362w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/arcade-cab-300x135.jpg 300w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/arcade-cab-1024x463.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 584px) 100vw, 584px" />](/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/arcade-cab.jpg)

Then the cabinet was milled out on a router and assembled on a Saturday.

<a style="font-style: inherit; line-height: 1.625;" href="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_131203.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-364" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_131203.jpg?w=584" alt="IMG_20130921_131203" width="584" height="778" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_131203.jpg 1536w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_131203-225x300.jpg 225w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_131203-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 584px) 100vw, 584px" /></a>

Here you can see the control panel going in, similar to the X-arcade. There&#8217;s room for a joystick and 6 buttons per player. We later modified this to accommodate extra buttons for coin drops and player buttons.<a style="font-style: inherit; line-height: 1.625;" href="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_132409.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-365" src="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_132409.jpg?w=584" alt="IMG_20130921_132409" width="584" height="778" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_132409.jpg 1536w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_132409-225x300.jpg 225w, /wp-content/uploads/2013/10/img_20130921_132409-768x1024.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 584px) 100vw, 584px" /></a>
